About This Era

The early 20th century marked the beginning of scientific inquiry into gut microorganisms. Researchers began to observe and document various bacteria in the human intestinal tract, though their specific functions remained largely unknown. Pioneering scientists laid the groundwork for understanding the complex relationship between gut microbes and human health.

Key Milestones Gallery

1908

Probiotics Concept

Élie Metchnikoff proposes that consuming beneficial bacteria could promote health and longevity

1928

Antibiotics Era

Alexander Fleming discovers penicillin, later leading to understanding of antibiotic impacts on gut flora

1953

Prebiotics Defined

Werner Kollath introduces the term "prebiotics" to describe compounds that promote beneficial gut bacteria

1982

H. pylori Connection

Marshall and Warren discover H. pylori bacteria's role in gastric ulcers and stomach cancer

2001

Human Microbiome

Joshua Lederberg coins the term "microbiome" to describe the ecological community of microorganisms

2008

Human Microbiome Project

NIH launches comprehensive project to characterize the human microbiome and its role in health

2013

FMT Recognition

Fecal Microbiota Transplantation gains FDA recognition for treatment of C. difficile infection

2020

Precision Probiotics

Emergence of tailored probiotics designed for specific health conditions and individual microbiomes
